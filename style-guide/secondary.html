Sonics arena a long shot

If it revives, however, the project will be significantly different than the one Hansen and the city imagined in 2012, perhaps with no public money and no NBA team.

The corpse seemed to pop to life last week with news that Hansen purchased the remaining five acres for the project for $32 million — about three times its assessed worth. The Seattle Times ran with a “full steam ahead” headline on the story, based on a quote from Bill Vipond, a longtime Seattle real estate consultant hired by Hansen.

Don't believe the hype

“I’ve never gotten any direction (from Hansen) other than full steam ahead, we’re bringing the Sonics back,” he said. “The general directive is, ‘Let’s go, let’s get the Sonics back, let’s keep working on getting the team.’”

But as it turned out, the purchase has long been part of the plan. Hansen included the land in his original presentation to the council years ago. He’s held an option on the property for more than three years, and merely wrote the check now ahead of the option’s expiration next year.
